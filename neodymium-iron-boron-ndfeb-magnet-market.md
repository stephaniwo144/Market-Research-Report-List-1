<p><p>The Neodymium-Iron-Boron (NdFeB) magnet market comprises two primary types: sintered NdFeB magnets and bonded NdFeB magnets. Sintered NdFeB magnets are produced through a powder metallurgy process, resulting in high-density and high-performance magnets suitable for various applications like motors and electronics. In contrast, bonded NdFeB magnets are made by combining magnetic powders with a polymer matrix, allowing for complex shapes and lower production costs, making them ideal for lightweight applications and parts requiring intricate designs.</p></p>
