<p><p>The buckwheat products market is categorized mainly into three types: unhulled buckwheat, raw buckwheat, and roasted buckwheat. Unhulled buckwheat retains its outer shell, providing high fiber and nutritional benefits but requires longer cooking. Raw buckwheat refers to the groats without the hull, appreciated for their versatility in cooking and as gluten-free options. Roasted buckwheat, often called kasha, is heat-treated to enhance flavor and aroma, commonly used in various dishes and snacks, appealing to health-conscious consumers.</p></p>
