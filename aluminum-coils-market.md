<p><p>The aluminum coils market is segmented into several series based on alloying elements. The 1xxx series, known for high corrosion resistance and good conductivity, is commonly used in electrical applications. The 2xxx series, with copper as a primary alloying element, offers superior strength but lower corrosion resistance, making it ideal for aerospace components. The 3xxx series, alloyed with manganese, provides moderate strength and workability for beverage cans. The 5xxx series, featuring magnesium, excels in marine environments. The 6xxx series, combined with magnesium and silicon, is versatile for structural applications, while the 7xxx series, primarily containing zinc, is favored for high-strength aerospace and automotive parts.</p></p>
