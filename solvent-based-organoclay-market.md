<p><p>The solvent-based organoclay market can be segmented into three primary types: meal, powder, and superfine powder. Meal is coarser and typically used in applications requiring a bulkier texture, such as in certain industrial uses. Powder is a finer variant offering improved dispersion in various solvents and is commonly utilized in coatings and sealants. Superfine powder, the finest type, provides exceptional properties for high-performance applications, enhancing performance in critical formulations like lubricants and plastics, where precision is essential.</p></p>
