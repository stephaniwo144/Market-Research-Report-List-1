<p><p>Bioabsorbable sutures are medical devices made from materials that can be naturally degraded and absorbed by the body over time. These sutures are commonly made from polymers such as polyglycolic acid and polylactic acid.</p><p>The Bioabsorbable Sutures Market is experiencing substantial growth, driven by an increasing number of surgical procedures worldwide, advancements in suture materials, and a rising preference for minimally invasive techniques. <p><p>The bioabsorbable sutures market comprises several types, including catgut, polymer chemical synthesis lines, and pure natural collagen sutures. Catgut, derived from animal intestines, naturally absorbs over time, making it suitable for absorbable applications. Polymer chemical synthesis sutures, manufactured from synthetic polymers, offer controlled degradation rates and enhanced biocompatibility. Pure natural collagen sutures, sourced from collagen, promote tissue healing and integration. Additionally, other market variants may include hybrid or composite materials, catering to specific surgical needs and preferences.</p></p>
