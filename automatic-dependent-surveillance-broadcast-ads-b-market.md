<p><p>Automatic Dependent Surveillance Broadcast (ADS-B) is a surveillance technology that enables aircraft to determine their position via satellite navigation and periodically broadcast it. The ADS-B market comprises three primary types: ADS-B Out, which transmits aircraft position and data to ground stations and other aircraft; ADS-B In, which receives and displays information from other aircraft and ground stations; and ADS-B Ground Stations, which collect and relay ADS-B signals for air traffic management, enhancing situational awareness for both pilots and controllers.</p></p>
