<p><p>The Automotive Oxygen Sensor market includes two primary types: Titanium Oxide and Zirconia sensors. Titanium Oxide sensors use a titanium dioxide element to measure oxygen levels in exhaust gases, offering faster response times and durability but requiring precise temperature control. Zirconia sensors, the more common type, utilize a zirconium dioxide ceramic element to generate a voltage based on oxygen concentration, providing high accuracy and reliability. Both types are essential for optimizing fuel efficiency and reducing emissions in modern vehicles.</p></p>
