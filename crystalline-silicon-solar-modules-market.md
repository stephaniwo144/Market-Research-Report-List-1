<p><p>The crystalline silicon solar modules market consists of two primary types: monocrystalline and polycrystalline solar modules. Monocrystalline solar modules are made from a single, continuous crystal structure, providing higher efficiency and a sleek aesthetic, making them ideal for space-constrained installations. In contrast, polycrystalline solar modules are crafted from multiple crystal fragments, offering a more cost-effective solution with slightly lower efficiency. Both types serve distinct market needs, with monocrystalline modules typically favored for residential applications and polycrystalline modules for commercial use.</p></p>
