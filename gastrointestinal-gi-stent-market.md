<p><p>The gastrointestinal (GI) stent market includes various types, each designed to address specific obstructions or diseases within the GI tract. Biliary stents facilitate bile flow, typically in cases of bile duct obstruction. Duodenal stents aid in food passage when there's a blockage in the duodenum. Colonic stents treat colonic obstructions, often used in colorectal cancer. Pancreatic stents manage pancreatic duct obstructions, while esophageal stents help maintain esophageal patency in conditions like strictures or tumors. Each type plays a crucial role in improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
