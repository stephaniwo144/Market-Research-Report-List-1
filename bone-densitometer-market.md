<p><p>The bone densitometer market consists of various types used for measuring bone mineral density. DEXA (Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry) is the most widely used method, known for its accuracy and low radiation exposure. Ultrasound densitometry utilizes sound waves to assess bone density, making it radiation-free. QCT (Quantitative Computed Tomography) offers three-dimensional imaging for precise bone analysis. Other methods include peripheral testing technologies and MRI-based assessments, each contributing to the growing landscape of bone health evaluation and osteoporosis screening.</p></p>
