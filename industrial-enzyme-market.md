<p><p>The industrial enzyme market is categorized into four main types: carbohydrases, proteases, lipases, and others. Carbohydrases are enzymes that break down carbohydrates, commonly used in food processing and biofuel production. Proteases facilitate protein breakdown, essential in the food industry and pharmaceuticals. Lipases target fats and oils, playing a crucial role in the dairy and biodiesel sectors. The "others" category includes various specialized enzymes that serve unique functions across industries like textiles, paper, and pharmaceuticals, enhancing efficiency and sustainability.</p></p>
