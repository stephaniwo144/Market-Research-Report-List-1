<p><ul><li>Alginates</li><li>Silicones</li><li>Polyethers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The impression materials market consists of three primary types: alginates, silicones, and polyethers. Alginates are natural, cost-effective materials used primarily for preliminary impressions due to their ease of use and flexibility. Silicones, including addition-cure and condensation-cure types, offer high accuracy and dimensional stability, making them suitable for detailed impressions. Polyethers provide exceptional detail and tear resistance, ideal for complex restorations. Each material serves specific dental applications, catering to various clinician preferences and patient needs in orthodontics and prosthodontics.</p></p>
